Waltz

A little motto of the dance style

One of the most popular ballroom dances, the Waltz is a smooth dance traveling around the line of dance. Its elegant rise and fall action with its turning, embracing couples makes waltz a mainstay of ballroom dancing. Today where you can find it in elegant settings such as ballroom and wedding venues. The Viennese waltz is a rapid, whirling variation.

Tango

A little motto of the dance style

One of the most famous and fascinating of all dances, the tango is a sensual ballroom dance that emphasizes the vibrant and playful style of movement, rich expressions, improvisation and requires close connection and passion between dancers.

The core concept of Tango revolves around the bold, sensual interaction between leader and follower. The leader through their classic embrace, invites the follower to a dance move. The follower then chooses how they will respond. The result of this flamboyant cooperative process can be an improvisational dance that captivates the dancers and spectators.

In Southwest Florida, Tango can be seen in USA Dance events and ballroom venues, such as dance studios.

Foxtrot

A little motto of the dance style

Foxtrot is extremely versatile and can be danced to a variety of musical styles and tempi. It’s a smooth progressive dance similar in look to waltz. The characteristic long, continuous flowing movements across the dance floor involve a subtle rise & fall action and traveling around the line of dance.

The Foxtrot is danced is danced in combinations of slow and quick steps, with each slow step taking two beats and each quick step one beat of music.

East Coast Swing

A little motto of the dance style

Swing is an energetic, fun spot dance, not moving with a line of dance. Free rhythmic interpretation is characteristic, using single, double or triple rhythms. East Coast Swing (or Swing), evolved from the Lindy Hop and is perhaps the most famous American dance. Best-known forms of Swing include the Jitter bug, Charleston and Shag, which consolidated into what is called the Lindy.

>Originating in the 1950’s in Harlem, swing was revived in the late 1990’s and early 2000’s by such bands as the Brian Setzer Orchestra and the Big Bad Voodoo Daddy. Today’s swing dance and its variations are popular around the world in social settings and dance competitions.

Rumba

A little motto of the dance style

Rumba is a romantic dance derived from a Cuban rhythm dance. The Rumba is characterized by a smooth, subtle hip motion and a heavy walking step. The basic steps and rhythm are relatively easy to learn, making it a great dance for beginners when the music slows down. Rumba’s tantalizing rhythm came to the United Sates in the early 1930s, and has remained one of the most popular social dances in Southwest Florida.

Cha Cha

A little motto of the dance style

Cha-Cha is sexy and fun for people of all ages. Originating in Cuba, Cha-Cha is named for the rhythm resulting from syncopation (the “cha-cha”). Cha-Cha’s tempo is anywhere from slow to fast and lively. It’s danced in place with compact steps and is very much an “on-the-beat” dance encouraging emotional expression. This freedom makes it a “let-it-all-out” type of dance.

Cha-Cha is a mainstay dance at Southwest Florida dance clubs, including our chapter events.
